Step-by-Step: Selling Products

1
Find the ProductType the name in the search box or scan the product barcode. Products appear as cards below.
2
Select Variant (if needed)If a product comes in different sizes/colours, a picker will pop up. Choose the right size and colour, then click Add.
3
Adjust CartUse + / − to change quantities. Click the discount icon on an item to apply a per-item discount. Use the cart discount field for overall discounts.
4
Attach Customer (Optional)Search for the customer in the cart panel. This tracks their purchase history.
5
CheckoutClick Checkout. Choose payment method, enter the amount received, and click Complete Sale. A receipt appears — print it if needed.

Payment Methods

MethodWhen to Use
💵 CashCustomer pays in cash — enter exact amount received, change is calculated
💳 CardDebit or credit card payment
📱 Mobile PaymentMobile banking transfer (e.g. FriMi)
🏦 Bank TransferDirect bank transfer
Stock Error? If a sale fails with "Insufficient sale stock", the item is fully reserved for rentals. Tell your manager — they can adjust the stock allocation in Inventory.

4 · Rental Bookings

Cashier   Manager

Creating a New Rental Booking

1
Go to Rentals → + New Rental
2
Search and Select the CustomerType their name or phone number. If they're new, create them first in Customers.
3
Set the Rental PeriodChoose Start Date (pickup date) and End Date (return date). The number of days is calculated automatically.
4
Add ItemsClick + Add Item, search for the product, pick the variant and quantity. Repeat for multiple items.
5
Set Advance PaymentEnter how much the customer is paying now. Select the payment method.
6
Save the BookingClick Create Booking. A booking number is generated (e.g. BK-0042) and a confirmation message is sent to the customer.

Updating Rental Status

When a customer picks up their items, you need to update the rental status:

  1. Go to Rentals, find the booking (search by name or booking number)
  2. Click the booking to open the detail view
  3. Click Update Status and select the new status
What's HappeningStatus to Set

Customer has collected the itemsPicked Up
Customer returned items, all paidCompleted
Booking is no longer neededCancelled
Kanban View Click the board icon in the top-right of the Rentals page to switch to Kanban view — it shows all bookings as cards grouped by status. Great for a quick visual overview.

Step-by-Step: Processing a Return

1
Find the RentalGo to Returns. Find the customer's booking — overdue ones show in red at the top.
2
Click Process ReturnA form opens showing all items in the rental.
3
Set the Return DateDefaults to today. Change only if the customer actually returned on a different date.
4
Check Each Item's ConditionFor every item, click one of the three condition tiles:
✓ Good
No extra charge
⚠ Damaged
Charge auto-added
✕ Lost
Full price charged
  • Good — Item is returned in acceptable condition. No extra charge.
  • Damaged — Item has visible damage. A damage fee is calculated automatically based on shop rules. The amount is shown as a read-only badge.
  • Lost — Item was not returned. If it's a sale item, its selling price is charged. For rental-only items, you can enter a custom charge amount.
5
Review the Charges SummaryAt the bottom you'll see: Late fine (if overdue) + Damage/Loss charges + Total to collect.
6
Select Payment Method and ConfirmChoose how the customer is paying the charges. Click Process Return to complete.
No Charges? If all items are Good and the return is on time, the charges summary will show LKR 0. Just confirm the return.
Late Fine The system calculates the late fine automatically based on how many days past the due date (minus the grace period set by your manager). You cannot override this from the Returns page — ask your manager if a fine needs to be waived.

6 · Products & Inventory

Inventory Staff   Cashier (view only)

Browsing Products

Go to Products to see the full catalog. You can search by name, SKU, or barcode, and filter by category or type (Rental / Sale / Both). Click on any product to see its full details, variants, and stock levels.

Recording Stock Movements (Inventory Staff)

📦 Inventory — Adjust Stock
Product · SKU · Total · For Sale · For Rent · Damaged
Blazer M Black · BLZ-M-BLK 8   3   5   0   Adjust
  1. Go to Inventory
  2. Find the product variant you need to update (use the search box)
  3. Click the Adjust button on that row
  4. Select the Movement Type:
    • Stock In — New stock arrived from supplier
    • Stock Out — Items removed (disposal, write-off)
    • Adjustment — Correcting count after physical stocktake
    • Mark Damaged — Recording items found damaged in store
  5. Enter the Quantity and a brief Reason
  6. Click Record Movement
Stock Columns Explained
For Sale = items you can sell at the counter
For Rent = items reserved for rental customers
Total = all physical items in the shop

Printing Barcode Labels

  1. Go to Products → click on a product
  2. In the Variants table, find the variant you need
  3. Click the 🖨 Barcode button in that row
  4. A preview shows the barcode and product details
  5. Set the number of copies (e.g. print 10 labels for new stock)
  6. Click Print — your browser's print dialog will open

7 · Customer Records

Cashier   Manager

Adding a New Customer

  1. Go to Customers → click + Add Customer
  2. Fill in: Full Name (required), Phone, Email, WhatsApp, Address
  3. Add any Notes (e.g. "prefers traditional styles", "VIP customer")
  4. Click Save Customer
WhatsApp If the customer has a different WhatsApp number than their main phone, fill in both. The system sends reminders to WhatsApp first.

Finding a Customer

Use the search bar to find customers by name, phone number, or email. Click their name to see full details including rental history and any outstanding fines (shown in red).

Outstanding Fines If a customer has an outstanding fine shown in red, remind them to settle it when they come to the counter for a new rental.

Applying for Leave

  1. Go to Employees → My Leaves tab
  2. Click + Apply for Leave
  3. Select Leave Type: Annual, Sick, Casual, or Unpaid
  4. Choose Start Date and End Date
  5. The number of days is calculated automatically
  6. Write a brief Reason
  7. Click Submit — your manager will be notified

Leave Status Guide

StatusMeaningWhat to Do
PendingSubmitted, awaiting manager decisionWait for approval. You can cancel if plans change.
ApprovedManager has approved your leaveYou're all set — the dates appear on the team calendar.
RejectedManager has declined your requestCheck the review note for the reason. Apply for different dates if needed.

Leave Calendar

Click the Calendar tab to see a monthly view of leave days. Your approved leaves show in green, pending leaves in amber. Use the arrow buttons to browse months.

9 · Notifications

Cashier   Manager

The Notifications page shows all messages sent to customers (booking confirmations, reminders, etc.) and lets you send manual messages when needed.

Sending a Manual Message

  1. Go to Notifications → click Send Notification
  2. Search for the customer by name or phone
  3. Choose the channel — WhatsApp (recommended), SMS, or Email
  4. Select the notification type (e.g. Return Reminder)
  5. Edit the message text if needed
  6. Click Send
Common Use Case Use this to send a friendly reminder to a customer whose rental is due tomorrow, or to confirm a pickup time.

10 · Reports

Cashier   Manager

Go to Reports in the sidebar to view business performance data. Five tabs are available:

TabWhat You'll See
OverviewRevenue chart and totals for the last 7/30/365 days
SalesTotal sales, top-selling products, payment methods
RentalsBooking counts, revenue, top rented items
InventoryStock levels by category, low stock alerts
FinesLate fines issued and collected, top late returners

Downloading a PDF Report

  1. Go to Reports and click the tab you want (e.g. Sales)
  2. Set the date range using the From/To date fields
  3. Click the Download PDF button in the top-right
  4. A PDF with charts and data tables will download automatically
CSV Export Many report sections also have a small Export CSV button next to the chart title. Use this to open the data in Excel for further analysis.
